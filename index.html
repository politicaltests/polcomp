The Interactive Political Compass

The Political Compass is a coordinate-based chart that aims to represent the political beliefs of different people. The compass is split into two axes: The Left-Right Axis, and the Authoritarian-Libertarian Axis.



What is the Political Compass?

The Political Compass is a political spectrum chart that places a person's political beliefs on a coordinate chart that has two axes. The two axes are the Economic and the Civil axes. The economic axis measures a person's preferred economic system, with Left on one end, and Right on the other. The civil axis measures the size of the government within a person's political views, with Authoritarian on one end, and Libertarian on the other. It categorizes people into four quadrants, based on their placements: Authoritarian Right, Authoritarian Left, Libertarian Left, and Libertarian Right.
